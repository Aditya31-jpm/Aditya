Buggati-Chiron

For the 1999 concept car and the car that originates this design, see Bugatti 18/3 Chiron and Bugatti Vision Gran Turismo. The Bugatti Chiron is a mid-engine two-seater sports car developed and manufactured in Molsheim, France by French automobile manufacturer Bugatti Automobiles S.A.S.. The successor to the Bugatti Veyron,[6] the Chiron was first shown at the Geneva Motor Show on 1 March 2016.[7][8] The car is based on the Bugatti Vision Gran Turismo concept car. The car is named after the Monegasque driver Louis Chiron.[9] The car shares the name with the 1999 Bugatti 18/3 Chiron concept car.
